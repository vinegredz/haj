Program

Justice Served

Helping individuals and changing the system

This program provides grants to sponsor the Israeli Supreme Court appeals of Jews who have received a verbal refusal at the Israeli consulate. Specifically, it is aimed at applicants who cannot afford the legal fees themselves. With grants of up to $10,000 per case, HELP A JEW fights for every Jew's legal AND God-given right to return home to the holy land.

Read more about how the Aliya process differs drastically for Western vs. Soviet Jews.

Program

Into the Archives

Resources to research FSU state records

This program provides grants for applicants who need the help of professional archivists. In the Former Soviet Union, state archives contain official records of copies of all of birth and death certificates, and those records contain the words,

"Ethnicity: Jew."

From Vladivostok to Minsk, HELP A JEW obtains documentation when the situation is complicated.
